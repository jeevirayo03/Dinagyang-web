History of Dinagyang Festival

The Dinagyang Festival is one of the most vibrant and celebrated festivals in the Philippines, held every fourth Sunday of January in Iloilo City. It is a religious and cultural festival in honor of the Santo Niño (Child Jesus), reflecting the deep faith of the Ilonggo people.

The festival traces its roots back to 1967, when a replica of the Santo Niño from Cebu was brought to Iloilo. Inspired by the Ati-Atihan Festival of Aklan, Iloilo developed its own unique celebration, later named Dinagyang, meaning “merrymaking” in Hiligaynon.

Dinagyang also commemorates the arrival of Malay settlers in Panay Island and their interaction with the indigenous Ati people. This is portrayed through the Ati Tribe Competition, where performers wear warrior costumes and body paint.

Today, Dinagyang is internationally recognized for its high-energy performances, creative choreography, and elaborate costumes, attracting thousands of tourists every year.
